Great Movements in Art History

14th–17th Century

Renaissance

The rebirth of classical ideals. Mastery of perspective, anatomy, and light.

Da Vinci · Michelangelo · Raphael

17th Century

Baroque

Dramatic contrasts of light and dark, emotional intensity, and grandeur.

Rembrandt · Caravaggio · Vermeer

1860s–1890s

Impressionism

Capturing fleeting moments in light and colour, liberated from tradition.

Monet · Renoir · Degas

1880s–1910s

Post-Impressionism

Emotion, structure, and symbolism took centre stage in raw personal work.

Van Gogh · Cézanne · Gauguin

1900s–1920s

Art Nouveau

Flowing organic lines and decorative ornamentation fusing fine and applied arts.

Klimt · Mucha · Toulouse-Lautrec

1907–1920s

Cubism

Shattering perspective into fragments. Multiple viewpoints on one canvas.

Picasso · Braque · Léger

1920s–1950s

Surrealism

Dreamscapes, the unconscious mind, and impossible imagery made real.

Dalí · Kahlo · Magritte

1905–1940s

Expressionism

Distortion and raw emotion over realistic depiction — the inner world visible.

Munch · Kirchner · Nolde

1940s–1960s

Abstract Expressionism

Gestural, emotional, monumental canvases that redefined modern painting.

Pollock · Rothko · de Kooning

1950s–1970s

Pop Art

Consumer culture reimagined as fine art. Bold, graphic, and deliberately commercial.

Warhol · Lichtenstein · Hockney

Early 20th Century

American Modernism

Vast landscapes, abstracted forms, and a new visual language for a new world.

O'Keeffe · Hopper · Sheeler

1960s–Present

Contemporary

Art without limits — installations, digital works, and street art.

Banksy · Kusama · Basquiat
