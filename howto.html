User Guide

How to Use

Everything you need to know — from navigating the gallery to painting your first masterwork in the Studio.

Mobile Experience

Exploring on Your Phone

Luminary Atelier is fully optimised for mobile — from the gallery grid to the creative Studio. Here's how to get the most out of it.

01

Tap the Hamburger Menu

The ☰ icon in the top-right opens navigation. Tap any page link to explore the gallery, artists, or Studio.

💡 Swipe right anywhere to close the menu

02

Browse the Gallery

Artworks display in a responsive 2-column grid. Tap any work to open the full lightbox view with title, artist, and description.

💡 Swipe left/right in lightbox to navigate works

03

Use Filter Chips

Scroll the filter bar horizontally to see all art movements. Tap a chip to filter the gallery instantly.

04

Open the Studio

Tap "Studio" in the menu. On mobile, tools appear in a scrollable bottom bar. Tap 🎨 to open the colour & brush panel.

💡 Double-tap the canvas to zoom to fit

05

Draw with Fingers

Touch and drag to draw. Pinch two fingers to zoom in/out on the canvas. Use two-finger pan to scroll around a zoomed canvas.

06

Save Your Artwork

Tap the 💾 save icon in the top bar to download your artwork. Choose PNG for best quality or JPEG for smaller files.

Tablet Experience

A Natural Canvas

Tablets offer the perfect balance — a large touch screen for exploring the gallery and a natural drawing surface for the Studio.

01

Navigation Sidebar

In landscape mode the nav links display as a sidebar. In portrait, use the hamburger menu.

💡 Rotate for a different layout experience

02

Gallery Grid

Artworks show in a 3-column grid. Tap to open lightbox. Swipe left/right to navigate between works.

03

Studio — Ideal Experience

The Studio shines on tablets. The left tool panel and right colour/brush panel are visible simultaneously.

💡 Use a stylus for the most natural painting feel

04

Pinch to Zoom

Pinch two fingers on the canvas to zoom in for detail work. Spread to zoom out. Two-finger drag to pan.

05

Layers Panel

Tap the Layers tab in the right panel to add, duplicate, or delete layers. Tap a layer to select it.

06

Artist Profiles

Tap any artist card to open their full profile — biography, notable works, and a notable fact.

Laptop Experience

Full Power at Your Fingertips

Laptops unlock the full Luminary experience — keyboard shortcuts, hover effects, and the complete Studio layout.

01

Hover Interactions

Hover over artist cards to reveal bios. Hover gallery items to see artwork info. The cursor glow follows your mouse.

02

Studio Keyboard Shortcuts

Press B for brush, E for eraser, Z to undo, Ctrl+S to save. Full shortcut table below.

💡 Press ? in Studio to see all shortcuts

03

Full Studio Layout

The left tool panel, main canvas, and right panels (Colour, Brush, Layers, Adjust) are all visible simultaneously.

04

Scroll to Zoom

In the Studio, use Ctrl + scroll wheel to zoom the canvas in/out. Hold Space + drag to pan around.

05

Gallery Search

Use the search bar to filter artworks by title, artist name, or year. Combine with movement filters.

06

Lightbox Navigation

In the gallery lightbox, use ← → arrow keys to navigate between works. Press Escape to close.

Desktop Experience

The Immersive View

On a large desktop monitor, Luminary Atelier opens up fully — wide-format gallery grids, expanded artist showcases, and the complete Studio in all its glory.

01

Hero & Animated Strip

The home page hero runs a scrolling art strip on the right-hand side — only visible at desktop widths for maximum impact.

02

5-Column Gallery Grid

At full desktop width the gallery displays 4–5 columns. Hover over works to reveal info overlays.

03

Artist Modal — Full Layout

Artist modals split into image on the left, bio and notable works on the right — fully utilising screen space.

04

Studio — Maximum Canvas

At desktop widths the canvas is at its largest. The full tool, colour, brush, layer, and adjust panels are all open by default.

💡 Use a drawing tablet + stylus for best results

05

Dual Monitor Tip

Open the gallery in one window and the Studio in another — reference artworks while you paint your own interpretation.

06

Zoom & Ruler

The Studio zoom percentage shows bottom-left. Use View → Show Rulers to enable canvas rulers for precise placement.
